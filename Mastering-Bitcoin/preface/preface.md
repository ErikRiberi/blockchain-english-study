# Writing the Bitcoin Book

## Summary

In this chapter, the author talks about how he discovered Bitcoin in 2011.
At first, he thought it was "nerd money" and ignored it.
Then, when he read the whitepaper by Satoshi Nakamoto, he understood that Bitcoin was a decentralized digital trust network.
Finally, he decided to work on Bitcoin and wrote this book.

# Why Are There Bugs on the Cover?

## Summary

This section explains why there are ants on the cover.
Leafcutter ants form a highly complex society, but each ant follows simple rules.
They do not eat leaves directly; instead, they use them to farm fungus, which is their main food source.
Even though they have a queen, there is no central authority.
Their complex behavior emerges from simple interactions between individuals.
In Bitcoin, the interaction between many nodes is what leads to the emergence of complexity and sophisticated behavior, like an ant colony of simple nodes following simple rules.

- Set of rules: Conjunto de reglas
